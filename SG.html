Modern SG (Shooting Guard): Roles, Responsibilities and Famous Players.

The shooting Guard is the position with fewest change in role since basketball was born. If PG is considered to be the conductor, SG is the musician of a team. Classic SGs are players who are a bit higher than PG but must possess impressive finishing and breakthrough skills. A shooting guard's main objective is to score points for their team. Some teams ask their shooting guards to bring up the ball as well. This article will introduce about roles of a variety SG models, in order to help you have clearer look about what position you play in.

1. Scoring Shooting Guards.

The Scoring Shooting Guard are SG players playing in a classic style focused on their only mission on the field: Scoring. Despite being called the classic style, the Scoring SGs are never outdated in any period of basketball tactics. These SGs are players with extremely diverse scoring skills. They are only slightly higher than PG so speed, technique and flexibility are the optimal weapons. Scoring SG can often throw both long range and average distance, and is also very dominant in the fastbreak or after opponent's error. The defensive mission is not a burden of Scoring SGS, but the superstars playing in this position often also have great defense capabilities.

Stars of Scoring SG position in NBA history have always been the players with the most fanbase because of their ability to make incrediblie shots. They become the symbols of the club or even the tournament. These can be representative players such as Michael Jordan, Kobe Bryant, Tracy McGrady or Vince Carter.

Michael Jordan, a Scoring SG, is considered the GOAT (greatest of all time) of the basketball world.

2. Slashing Shooting Guards.

Like Slashing PG, Slashing SGs are players like holding ball and breaking the opponent's defense. Their main task is still to score, but they have more ability to break through defenses than Scoring SGs. With his breakthroughs, Slashing SG disrupted the defensive position of the opponent, thereby creating more space for players outside the 3-point line or the Big men in the paint area. Slashing SGs are usually players with excellent speed and power. These abilities help them develop their skills to crossover. Slashing SGs are not afraid of collision and very strong in the final shots near the basket. They play like "suicide soldiers", are not afraid of the Big Men defending under the opponent's basket and often catch a lot of fouls.

In modern basketball, the Slashing SG accounts for a small number but always the key in the tactics of the team that owns this player model. The most successful players in this role over the past few years are Dwayne Wade and Manu Ginobili.

Dwyane Wade, the greatest Slashing SG of all time.

3. 3-point Specialist Shooting Guards.

In basketball, 3-point shots always bring extreme excitement to the audience. Of the 5 positions played on the basketball court, perhaps no other produces more shooters than the SG position. A 3pt Specialist can be compared to a sniper assassin. Sniper assassin never has to join in battle. Their job is to hide from above and finish the opponent with a single shot. So are the sniper assassins in the SG position, they constantly move outside the 3-point line, read the match and occupy the gaps, receive the ball from their teammates and score points with instant shots from any location.

The most important thing for 3pt Specialist is the ability to calm, assertive and confidence when throwing the hand, because just a little hesitant or distracting is an incorrect throw. They are also the coach's top priorities in making important shots at the end of the match. The famous 3pt Specialist players can be listed by Ray Allen, Reggie Miller, Klay Thopmson, Steve Kerr ...

Klay Thompson is clearly the most typical model of 3pt Specialist Shooting Guards. He is holding the record of most 3pt shot made in a single game of 14 successful shots

4. Defensive Shooting Guards.

If the SG position is the main scorer of a team, the Defensive Specialist SG is born to minimize the ability to score of the star SGs on the opponent's side. This is a SG model that possess stamina, stubbornness as well as the ability to focus on defending the opponent on the other side. They are often players who play extremely uncomfortably, sometimes have psychological attacks on your team players.

SGs of this type are often not so prominent in their ability to attack. When attacking they usually only have the task of stretching the defense or shooting open shots. The most well-known Defensive SGs are Bruce Bowen, Tony Allen, Iman Shumpert ...

Bruce Bowen may be the least fair-play player of the NBA history.
