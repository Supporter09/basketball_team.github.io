Modern C (Center): Roles, Responsibilities and Famous Players.

Center is marked with the number "5" on the coach's table. This is the type of player who has the best height and fitness on the pitch of the team so of course they have the technique as well as the resilience and speed that are inferior to the rest of the pitch. Although slow and inflexible, the centers often have a muscular superiority compared to the rest on the field. They are the players who have the most collisions on the pitch. In classic basketball tactics, the center position is the most important position on the pitch. Owning a talented cener can make the team play a lot easier.

1. Classic Centers.

Like the classic PF, Classic Center is the model in the original mission that this position was born. These centers own the superior bodybuilding and height, but are inflexible and manage quite slowly. They operate mainly near the trapezoidal area with the main task of rebounding as much as possible. A good Classic center must have more than 10 rebounds in every match he plays. In addition to the ability to rebound, the skills to score in a position to turn away from the basket in the trapezoidal area are also very important for classic strikers.

In the old days of basketball tactics, the classic center model appeared in every NBA team. Sometimes, this position determines the success of a team. In the glorious period of these centers, there was no team in the NBA basketball tournament survive without the appearance of great stars playing in the center. These include Wilt Chamberlain, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Bill Russell, Shaquille O'Neal, Hakeem Olajuwon ....

Center Kareem Abdul-Jabbar is the no.1 scorer in NBA history with 38,387 points throughout his career.

2. Rim Protector Center.

In today's NBA basketball tournament, the Rim Protectors don't need to be very muscular. They often have long arms, good jumping ability and the ability to read smart situations as well as choose the right time to prevent the opponent's finishing moves. The lack of muscle is compensated by the flexibility and agility that help them keep up with the pace of modern matches, support teammates defending and can include the position of Wings Players (the players operate the outer ring) as needed.

During the attack, the Rim Protectors did not take on a too large role. Their main task is to squash and rebounds. While unlike the classic center, a Rim Protector still guarantees a good rebound mission thanks to his rebound and long arm span. The outstanding Rim Protector in the current NBA basketball tournament can be mentioned with Rudy Gobert, Hassan Whiteside, Jarrett Allen, DeAndre Jordan, Bismack Byombo ...

Utah Jazz is having great advantages by owning the back-to-back DPOY Rudy Gobert on its rotation.

3. Versatile Centers.

Just like the PF position, nowadays centers have to change their playing style to match the flow of basketball tactics. The Versatile Center is still the biggest players on the court, but they have far more diverse skills than the traditional center striker. Unlike traditional players who rely on muscles and the ability to play near baskets, today's Versatile centers play with a wider range of skills that were previously difficult to find in a center. Most of these centers have good average throwing ability, even some players like Brook Lopez, Joel Embiid or DeMarcus Cousins ​​can throw from the 3rd line well.

The talented VersatilityCenter talented in the NBA basketball tournament today are mostly young players who possess extremely comprehensive skills. Stars who can rebound and defend well, score close to the basket but can still throw long range such as Karl-Anthony Towns, Myles Tuner or Joel Embiid will be the trend of the centers in the NBA basketball tournament. in the near future.

Joel Embiid is arguably the current best center in the NBA. Beside the habit to have ”trashtalk” on and off court, he is known for a very flexible play style

4. Defensive Point Guards.

This is an extremely rare form of dribbling defender in previous and current NBA history. The defender of this type has the ability to defend extremely well. They have the main task of stopping opponent’s stars from scoring. These PGs often possess great physical strength as well as the ability to concentrate highly during the game. On the offensive front, they usually only have simple passes to players who have the ability to create or score better.

Sometimes they also finish themselves at the opportunity, however their main task is still defense. They are also the start of the home team's counterattack thanks to their ability to steal the ball very quickly. The most famous defensive PG in history is obviously Gary "The Glove" Payton. In NBA today there are still some defensive PG models such as Patrick Berverley or Dante Exum.

Gary Payton is the most famous defensive PG in the NBA history
